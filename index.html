Creative Exploration on the Web   |   Assignment 1: Bauhaus

In looking through the collection, two pieces initially caught my immediate attention: "Jocular Sounds" (1929) by Wassily Kandinsky and the "Table Lamp" (1924) by Wilhelm Wagenfeld and Carl Jacob Jucker.

What drew me to both was a sense of completeness there seemed to be to each piece. Yet underlying that, the question I found myself coming back to was how the various elements within each related to one another and in turn served to contribute to that sense of wholeness I perceived—and then how to express that visually on the screen.

With that in mind, my process of sketching the elements however soon drifted from inspired to a bit haphazard to dull. And the lamp simply looked like the lamp (to an extent!), just on a table. So that was that. But SOMEHOW I needed to bring this thing to life!

The basic observance that a lamp after all does turn on and off, and objects look differently at night versus the day, led me to play around with that concept. And the fish, well… they were a bit of an afterthought. While I had overlooked ”Fish” by Joan Stack at first, what I liked about it was that they were creatures that could be animated, and the diamonds were fun as well. I also have a bit of distrust of fish, and find them cool but creepy!

On the technical side, some the animations were a bit challenging-—especially when shifting the backgrounds and adding new elements to the piece I had not planned on, which entailed having to reevaluate the order in which elements were drawn and to feel that some of the code had (has) to be superfluous. The other thing too, is that I hope the animations are evident! I look forward to learning more about ways to approach this in the next few weeks!

TITLE: There's somethin' fishy happenin' here!
